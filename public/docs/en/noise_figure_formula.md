### 1. Terminology Detailed Explanation
- **Noise Figure (NF)**: Represented in **dB**, it is the core metric for noise performance of RF/microwave components (like amplifiers, receivers). It indicates how much more noise the component produces compared to an ideal noiseless component. Lower NF means better performance (ideal is 0 dB).
- **Noise Temperature (Te, Equivalent Noise Temperature)**: Represented in **K** (Kelvin), it equates the internal noise to the thermal noise generated by a resistor at temperature **Te**. Lower Te means less noise.
- **Reference Temperature (To = 290 K)**: International standard reference temperature (IEEE Std 197-1957). Represents thermal noise baseline at room temperature (~17°C).

### 2. Conversion Formulas (KaTeX Precise Representation)
Conversions are based on the **Noise Factor F** (linear value):

\[
F = 1 + \frac{T_e}{T_0}
\]

Noise Figure (dB) is defined as:

\[
NF(\text{dB}) = 10 \log_{10} F = 10 \log_{10} \left(1 + \frac{T_e}{T_0}\right)
\]

Reverse solving for Noise Temperature:

\[
T_e = T_0 \left(10^{NF/10} - 1\right)
\]

### 3. Explanation of Principles
Noise figure stems from **Thermal Noise (Johnson-Nyquist noise)** theory: any resistor at temp T produces a noise power spectral density of \(kT\).
IEEE standardized "To = 290 K" input noise so components from different vendors can be compared.
- Ideal component: NF = 0 dB, Te = 0 K.
- Real components: NF > 0, Te > 0.
- In LNAs, satellite receivers, 5G/Radar, NF ↔ Te conversion is heavily used for cascaded system total noise calculation (Friis formula).
